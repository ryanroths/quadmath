How these numbers are calculated

Top speed comes from prop pitch speed: each prop rotation theoretically moves the quad forward by one "pitch length." We multiply that by estimated motor RPM (KV × voltage × ~0.85 efficiency factor to account for slip and load) and convert to mph.

Flight time is modeled from your battery capacity, an 80% usable discharge window (to protect cell health), and an estimated average current draw based on frame size and motor specs — tuned for aggressive freestyle flying rather than gentle cruising.

Thrust-to-weight uses community static-thrust benchmarks for common motor/prop/cell combinations at each frame size, since true thrust requires a bench test rig. A ratio above 2:1 is generally freestyle-capable; whoops often run lower due to prop and motor constraints at their scale.

Frame size reference

65mm whoops typically run 0802–1102 motors at 18-30K KV on 1S, with 31mm-40mm props. 75mm whoops step up slightly to 1103-1404 motors, often on 2S, with 40mm props for more punch.

3" freestyle bridges micro and mini quads — 1404-1606 motors, 2-4S, built for tight indoor/outdoor freestyle. 5" freestyle is the standard competition size — 2207-2306 motors on 4-6S. 7" long range prioritizes efficiency — larger props, lower KV, optimized for distance over raw punch.
